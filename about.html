Basics

Understand stock market fundamentals:

  • Stock exchanges: BSE, NSE.
  • Types of stocks: Common, Preferred.
  • Market participants: Investors, Brokers, Institutions.
  • Indices: NIFTY 50, Sensex.

Example: Buying 10 shares of Infosys at market price.

Key Formulas:

  • Market Capitalization = Share Price × Total Shares
  • EPS = Net Profit ÷ Number of Outstanding Shares

Glossary

  • Market Capitalization: Total value of company shares.
  • P/E Ratio: Price to Earnings = Share Price ÷ EPS
  • Dividends: Portion of profit shared with shareholders.
  • Liquidity: Ability to quickly buy/sell stocks without affecting price.

Analysis

Two main approaches:

  • Technical Analysis: Study of past price movements and patterns.
  • Fundamental Analysis: Evaluates financial health using balance sheet, income statement, cash flow.

Formulas:

  • Moving Average (MA) = Sum of Closing Prices ÷ Number of Periods
  • Return on Equity (ROE) = Net Profit ÷ Shareholder Equity × 100%
  • P/E Ratio = Market Price per Share ÷ EPS

Stock Risk

  • Systematic Risk: Market-wide risk affecting all stocks.
  • Unsystematic Risk: Company-specific risk.
  • Volatility measures how much stock price fluctuates.

Formulas:

  • Portfolio Risk (σp) = √(w1²σ1² + w2²σ2² + 2w1w2Cov(1,2))
  • Sharpe Ratio = (Return of Portfolio − Risk-Free Rate) ÷ Portfolio Std. Dev.

Derivatives

Financial instruments derived from underlying assets:

  • Futures: Obligation to buy/sell at predetermined price.
  • Options: Right, not obligation, to buy/sell.
  • Used for hedging or speculation.

Formulas:

  • Call Option Payoff = Max(0, Stock Price − Strike Price)
  • Put Option Payoff = Max(0, Strike Price − Stock Price)

Mutual Funds

  • Types: Equity, Debt, Hybrid funds.
  • NAV (Net Asset Value) = (Total Assets − Total Liabilities) ÷ Number of Units
  • Diversification reduces risk.
  • SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) helps in long-term wealth creation.
